Vocation

Each vocation is a unique way of giving one’s life to God through prayer, service, mission, and faithful witness in society.

A LIFE GIVEN TO GOD

Our Vocation

  • The vocation of the Servite Mission Sisters is a call to consecrated life within society, lived through prayer, service, community, and faithful witness in the midst of the people.
  • Through the evangelical counsels of poverty, chastity, and obedience, members dedicate their lives entirely to God while remaining actively present within society.
  • We seek to bring the light of Christ into the realities of everyday life through compassionate witness and service.
  • We welcome those who feel drawn to explore this way of life and discern whether God may be calling them to this vocation.

Discernment

Discernment is a journey of prayerfully seeking God’s will and listening attentively to His call.

  • Pray regularly
  • Participate in the sacramental life of the Church
  • Learn about the Institute and its mission
  • Seek spiritual guidance where appropriate
  • Reflect with sincerity and openness

Journey of Formation

Formation is a gradual process of spiritual growth, maturity, and deeper understanding of the vocation and mission.

Stage One

Candidacy

1–2 years of introduction, discernment, and initial formation.

Stage Two

Formation Period

2–3 years of deeper growth, commitment, and integration into daily life.

Stage Three

Profession

A formal commitment to God through poverty, chastity, and obedience.

✝️ Friars

Men living their vocation in community through priesthood, brotherhood, and pastoral ministry.

⛪ Monks

Men dedicated to a life of prayer, contemplation, and monastic community.

🙏 Contemplative Sisters

Women dedicated to silence, prayer, and contemplation in community.

🤝 Apostolic Sisters

Women serving through education, healthcare, and missionary outreach.

👥 Secular Institute Sisters

Women living their vocation within society through witness and service.

❤️ Lay Servite Members

Men and women living Servite spirituality in family and professional life.
